1. What is the London Food Wheel?

London Food Wheel is a random restaurant and dish picker built for the UK capital. It highlights the city's defining meals — from fish and chips and pie and mash to curries on Brick Lane, market meals by the Thames, and dining districts across the city.

2. Who should use this wheel?

Londoners who need a fast meal choice, visitors exploring the city, and anyone tired of endless restaurant browsing. Spin and let the capital's food scene make the decision for you.

3. How it works

Pick a preset then hit Spin. The wheel lands on a suggestion and announces it. Use it as a category prompt to choose a dish, restaurant style, or neighbourhood, then head out or order in. Spin again if it lands somewhere inconvenient.

4. Features

  • Four city presets: London Classics, International Eats, Trending Street Food, and Neighbourhood Picks.
  • Real London picks: Every item is grounded in an actual London dish, neighbourhood, or cuisine trend.
  • Mobile-friendly canvas wheel: Spins smoothly on phones, tablets, and desktops — perfect for deciding on the go.
  • Instant result: Winner appears clearly after every spin, so you can act fast.
  • Customisable colours: Use Local Brand Studio to theme the wheel in London red and navy.

5. London food categories

London Classics covers the dishes the city is famous for — fish and chips, roast dinner, pie and mash, full English breakfast, afternoon tea, sticky toffee pudding, steak and kidney pie, and bangers and mash. International Eats reflects London’s global food scene — Brick Lane curry, Southall biryani, Chinatown dim sum, ramen in Shoreditch, sushi in Mayfair, Greek Street souvlaki, and Italian in Soho. Trending Street Food is perfect for market meals — Borough Market tacos, Southbank street food, Camden bao, Portobello falafel, Greenwich pie, Spitalfields wraps, Notting Hill mash, and on-the-go delivery picks. Neighbourhood Picks maps dining districts — Covent Garden pasta, Kensington brunch, Shoreditch burgers, Soho tapas, Mayfair seafood, Peckham jerk chicken, Islington pizza, and West End steakhouse.

6. Tips

Use London Classics when you want a British comfort meal, International Eats for global cuisine, Trending Street Food for market-style dining, and Neighbourhood Picks when you want to pick a neighbourhood and explore it. Add your own favourite London restaurants to personalize the wheel further.

7. Alternatives

For a broader global food choice, try What Should I Eat Wheel. If you want a cuisine-specific suggestion, use the Indian, Chinese, or Persian food wheels. To pick a drink for your meal, try What Drink Should I Choose.

8. Why it works

London's food scene is enormous and varied, which makes choosing hard. This wheel turns that variety into a quick, fun decision with a practical local angle — one spin, one delicious direction.
