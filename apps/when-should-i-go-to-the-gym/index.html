1. What is the Gym Wheel?

The When Should I Go to the Gym Wheel is a random workout schedule picker that removes the only thing standing between you and the gym: the decision. Procrastination, analysis paralysis, and the eternal "I'll start Monday" loop are no match for a spin wheel. It selects your gym time and you go. Simple.

2. Who should use this wheel?

Anyone who has said "I'll go tomorrow" more than twice this week. People who have a gym membership and haven't used it since January. Anyone who spends more time researching the optimal workout schedule than actually working out. If the wheel tells you to go right now and you're reading this instead of going — that's the target audience.

3. How it works

Choose one of the four presets, then hit Spin. The wheel lands on your gym time and announces it. The social contract is now sealed. You have to go. Spin again only if you genuinely can't honour the result — and then go on the second spin, no exceptions.

5. Wheel categories

The Real Talk is the honest preset — "Right Now!", "After This Episode", "Tomorrow", "Monday Fresh Start", "Next Month", "When Motivated", "First of the Month", and the fan-favourite "After This Snack". Realistic Schedule is for when you want to actually commit to something — Mon/Wed/Fri, Tue/Thu/Sat, weekdays only, weekends, every other day, three times a week, daily, or the wildcard "Whenever I Feel Like It". Best Times to Go covers when science and gym-goer wisdom say workouts actually happen — 6am before work, lunch break, right after work, early evening, late night for the empty-gym crowd, weekend morning, midday, or "When the Gym is Empty". Motivation Mode is for when you need a push — "RIGHT NOW!", "Stop Scrolling — Go!", "No Excuses Today", "Before Dinner", "Champion Mode", "Power Hour Time", "This Is the Sign", and "Just This Once" (which we both know becomes a habit).

6. Tips

Use The Real Talk for maximum honesty, Realistic Schedule when you want to build an actual routine, Best Times to Go when you need to find your optimal training window, and Motivation Mode when you just need someone — or something — to tell you to get up and go. Add your own time slots for a personalised gym wheel. Honour every spin. That is the deal.

7. Alternatives

If the wheel sends you to the gym and you need motivation for what to eat after, try What Should I Eat Wheel for a post-workout meal. Still can't decide on anything? The Yes or No Wheel can settle whether you're actually going today. If you want to reward yourself after the gym, What Snack Should I Have has your recovery snack sorted.

8. Why it works

The hardest part of going to the gym is deciding to go. Once you're there, you'll work out. The decision — not the workout — is where people get stuck. A spin wheel sidesteps the whole mental negotiation by outsourcing the choice entirely. The wheel said go. You go. It works because it removes you from the equation at the exact moment you're most likely to talk yourself out of it. One spin, no excuses, let's go.
