    1. What Is El Gordo (Spanish Christmas Lottery)?

    El Gordo ("The Fat One") is the nickname for the top prize of the Spanish Christmas Lottery (Lotería de Navidad), the oldest and largest lottery draw in the world. Unlike most lotteries where you choose numbers, every ticket in the Spanish Christmas Lottery has a pre-printed 5-digit number from 00000 to 99999. There are 100,000 unique numbers, each printed in multiple series.

    This generator randomly selects one of those 100,000 numbers using crypto.getRandomValues() — the same cryptographic random number generator browsers use for security-sensitive operations — instead of Math.random(). Rejection sampling eliminates modulo bias, ensuring every number from 00000 to 99999 has an equal chance of being selected.

    The Spanish Christmas Lottery has been held since 1812, making it the world's oldest continuous lottery. The draw takes place every year on December 22nd and is broadcast live across Spain. The top prize, El Gordo, awards €4,000,000 per full ticket (€400,000 per décimo, the standard tenth-share).

    4. How Does the Number Generator Work?

    This generator uses crypto.getRandomValues() with rejection sampling to produce a uniformly random number between 0 and 99,999. The result is zero-padded to 5 digits (e.g., 04267) to match the official ticket format. Each generation is an independent event — there is no state carried between draws, and the full 00000–99999 range is covered with equal probability.

    9. What Is the History of the Spanish Christmas Lottery?

    The Spanish Christmas Lottery (Lotería de Navidad) has been held since 1812, making it the world's oldest continuous lottery. It is run by Loterías y Apuestas del Estado and has become an integral part of Spanish Christmas tradition.

    The draw uses two large revolving spheres: one containing 100,000 wooden balls (each with a unique 5-digit number) and another with 1,807 prize balls. Students from the San Ildefonso school sing the winning numbers as they are drawn — a tradition that dates back centuries and is broadcast live across Spain.

    The total prize pool is 70% of ticket sales, making it the most generous lottery in the world by total payout. The draw is not just a lottery — it is a national event that brings families and communities together every December 22nd.
