    1. What Is El Gordo (Spanish Christmas Lottery)?

    El Gordo ("The Fat One") is the nickname for the top prize of the Spanish Christmas Lottery (Lotería de Navidad), the oldest and largest lottery draw in the world. The primary keyword is El Gordo number generator, and it replicates the official ticket format: a 5-digit number from 00000 to 99999.

    Unlike most lotteries where you choose numbers, in the Spanish Christmas Lottery every ticket has a pre-printed number. There are 100,000 unique numbers, each printed in multiple series. This generator randomly selects one of those 100,000 numbers for you.

    No sign-up is required. Generate your number, copy it, and have fun dreaming about the €400,000 top prize per décimo.

    4. How the Spanish Christmas Lottery Works

    The Spanish Christmas Lottery draw takes place every year on December 22nd. It is run by Loterías y Apuestas del Estado and has been held since 1812, making it the world's oldest continuous lottery.

    The draw uses two large revolving spheres: one containing 100,000 wooden balls (each with a unique 5-digit number) and another with 1,807 prize balls. Students from the San Ildefonso school sing the winning numbers as they are drawn — it is a beloved national tradition broadcast live across Spain.

    The top prize, "El Gordo," awards €4,000,000 per full ticket (€400,000 per décimo, the standard tenth-share). The total prize pool is 70% of ticket sales, making it the most generous lottery in the world by total payout.

    This generator replicates the full 00000–99999 number range so your pick matches any real ticket in the draw.
