Two Truths and a Lie: Live Voting Icebreaker for Remote Teams

Two Truths and a Lie is a live room designed for team icebreakers and meetings. Host the round, let teammates vote, then reveal the lie together—making every session interactive, collaborative, and easy to run.

Why teams choose this room

  • Real-time participation: Everyone votes at the same time in a shared experience.
  • Host-led flow: The host controls setup, voting, and reveal for clear meeting rhythm.
  • Engaging icebreaker: It brings energy to remote onboarding, standups, and team-building sessions.

1. What is Two Truths and a Lie?

Two Truths and a Lie is a hosted live room where one person shares three statements and the group votes on which one is false. The reveal happens together, making it a collaborative and engaging team activity.

2. Who should use Two Truths and a Lie?

Use this game for remote onboarding, team warming, icebreakers, or fun standups. It works best for teams that want a quick shared experience that encourages storytelling and connection.

3. How Two Truths and a Lie works

The host creates a room, enters three statements, and marks one as the lie. Participants join, vote on the false statement, and the host reveals the answer when everyone is ready.

4. Two Truths and a Lie features

  • Hosted live room: Keeps the experience centralized and easy to manage.
  • Statement voting: Everyone participates by selecting the lie.
  • Reveal controls: The host decides when to close voting and show the answer.
  • Participant list: Tracks who has joined and who is voting.

5. Two Truths and a Lie use cases

This tool is perfect for team introductions, virtual happy hours, sprint kickoffs, and weekly check-ins where you want to build rapport and keep everyone engaged.

6. Two Truths and a Lie tips

Encourage honest, fun statements, keep the lie plausible, and give participants enough time to read all options before voting. Use the host notes to prepare rounds ahead of time for smoother meetings.

7. Alternatives to Two Truths and a Lie

For simpler icebreakers, try Random Name Picker. If you want more collaborative guessing games, Yes or No Wheel is another fun choice.

8. Why Two Truths and a Lie works

Two Truths and a Lie works because it combines social sharing with collective decision-making. The voting process makes it easy for teams to participate and discover new facts about each other in a low-pressure setting.
