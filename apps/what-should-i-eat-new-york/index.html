1. What is the New York Food Wheel?

New York Food Wheel is a random restaurant and dish picker built around the real NYC dining scene. It covers the city's culinary identity — from the legendary pizza slice and pastrami on rye that define New York food culture, to the immigrant neighbourhoods across all five boroughs that have made the city one of the most extraordinary places to eat on the planet.

2. Who should use this wheel?

New Yorkers who can't pick between a thousand excellent options, tourists trying to experience the city's iconic food scene, and anyone who has stood in Midtown or the Village completely stumped by too many great choices. Spin, walk, eat.

3. How it works

Choose one of the four presets, then hit Spin. The wheel lands on a pick and announces it. Use it as a category prompt — then find the best spot near you for that dish or style. Spin again if the pick is in the wrong borough.

4. Features

  • Four city presets: NYC Icons, Manhattan Dining, Borough Bites, and Street Food.
  • Real NYC picks: Every item is grounded in an actual New York food tradition, neighbourhood, or iconic institution.
  • Mobile-friendly canvas wheel: Spins smoothly on phones, tablets, and desktops — ideal for deciding on the go.
  • Instant result: Winner is shown clearly after every spin so you can act on it fast.
  • Customisable colours: Use Local Brand Studio to theme the wheel in NYC yellow cab gold or subway map colours.

5. NYC food categories

NYC Icons covers the dishes that define New York — the dollar pizza slice, Katz's pastrami on rye, bagel with lox and cream cheese, New York cheesecake, hot dog from a cart, Buffalo wings, the Dominique Ansel Cronut, and the black and white cookie. Manhattan Dining reflects the city's world-class restaurant scene — steakhouses in the Meatpacking District, sushi omakase in the West Village, French bistros on the Upper East Side, oyster bars in Midtown, tasting menu dinners, and rooftop bar bites. Borough Bites is New York at its most authentic — Chinatown dumplings on Mott Street, Flushing soup dumplings, Jackson Heights biryani, East Harlem tacos, Arthur Avenue pasta in the Bronx, Sunset Park tacos in Brooklyn, Brighton Beach borscht, and Flatbush jerk chicken. Street Food is the beating heart of NYC eating — halal cart chicken-over-rice, the dollar slice, a knish, a soft pretzel, a dirty water hot dog, a bodega egg & cheese sandwich, and a deli sandwich piled high.

6. Tips

Use NYC Icons for a classic tourist-bucket-list meal, Borough Bites when you want to eat like a real New Yorker, Manhattan Dining for a special occasion or expense account dinner, and Street Food when you want something fast, cheap, and deeply satisfying. Add your own favourite NYC spots for a fully personalised city food wheel.

8. Why it works

New York City has more restaurants than any other city in the United States, and a cultural diversity that puts food from every corner of the world within a subway ride.
