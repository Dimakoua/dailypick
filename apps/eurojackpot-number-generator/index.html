    1. What Is the EuroJackpot Number Generator?

    The EuroJackpot Number Generator is a free, browser-based quick pick tool that creates random number combinations matching the official EuroJackpot format: 5 main numbers from 1–50 and 2 Euro Numbers from a separate 1–12 pool. Every pick is statistically equivalent to quick picks generated by official lottery terminal machines across 18 participating European countries.

    I built this because most online generators use Math.random(), which isn't cryptographically secure. This tool uses crypto.getRandomValues() with Fisher-Yates shuffling and rejection sampling to eliminate modulo bias — ensuring every number has an equal chance of being selected. The two Euro Numbers are drawn from their own independent pool, guaranteeing they are always unique.

    EuroJackpot is drawn every Tuesday and Friday. The jackpot starts at €10 million and can reach the capped maximum of €120 million. Tickets are sold through national lottery operators in participating countries including Germany, Italy, Finland, Denmark, and others.

    3. What Are the EuroJackpot Rules?

    EuroJackpot uses a two-pool format with two distinct bonus numbers. Players pick 5 main numbers from a pool of 1–50 and 2 Euro Numbers from a separate, independent pool of 1–12. Both pools are drawn independently, and the two Euro Numbers are always different from each other.

    • Main pool: 5 unique numbers from 1–50
    • Euro Number pool: 2 unique numbers from 1–12 (separate pool)
    • Draw days: Tuesday and Friday
    • Prize tiers: 12 total
    • Jackpot win: Match all 5 main numbers + both Euro Numbers
    • Minimum win: Match 2 main numbers + 1 Euro Number (Tier 12)

    4. How Does the Number Generator Work?

    This generator uses crypto.getRandomValues() — the same cryptographic random number generator browsers use for security-sensitive operations — instead of Math.random(). For the main numbers, it builds a full pool of 1–50, applies a Fisher-Yates shuffle seeded by cryptographic randomness, and takes the first 5 values. For the Euro Numbers, it independently draws 2 unique values from 1–12 using the same process. Rejection sampling eliminates modulo bias, and each line is an independent draw.
