    How to Play Momentum Mayhem

    Objective

    The goal of the game is to work together with other players to push the red ball into all of the moving blue traps. The game is over when every trap has captured the ball.

    Controls

    Simply click anywhere on the game canvas. A force will be applied to the ball, pushing it away from your click. The closer you click to the ball, the stronger the push.

    The Traps

    The blue circles on the screen are the traps. Each trap is assigned to a player name. When the ball enters a trap, that player's name is added to the "Captured Players" list, and the trap disappears.

    Special Effects

    Capturing a trap can trigger a random special effect that changes the game dynamics for a short period. These include:

    • Speed Boost: The ball's speed is dramatically increased.
    • Size Change: The ball becomes either much larger or smaller.
    • Trap Frenzy: All remaining traps move at a much faster speed.

    Multiplayer

    This game is designed for collaboration! Share the session URL with your friends to have them join your game. All players control the same ball in real-time. You can customize the names of the players/traps in the settings menu.

    1. What Is Momentum Mayhem?

    Momentum Mayhem is a browser-based collaborative physics game designed for team building and meeting energizers. It focuses on teamwork and fast-paced interaction by having everyone push one ball toward traps assigned to players.

    The primary keyword is Momentum Mayhem, and the game offers a shared multiplayer experience that is easy to start and simple to play.

    Players join the same session URL, enter names, and then take turns clicking to influence the ball. Each captured trap records a teammate’s name, making the game both competitive and cooperative.

    2. How to Use It?

    1. Open Momentum Mayhem and copy the session URL to invite teammates.
    2. Enter or update player names in Settings, then start the game from the main screen.
    1. Click anywhere on the canvas to push the ball. The closer your click, the stronger the force.
    1. Watch the ball move through the field, capture traps, and fill the captured player list.

    3. Key Features & Customization

    • Real-time multiplayer: all players share control of the same ball, making it a collaborative experience.
    • Simple click controls: click near the ball to push it, with intuitive physics and responsive gameplay.
    • Player capture system: each trap is linked to a teammate name, so captures become part of the team story.
    • Session sharing: invite teammates with a unique session URL and continue the same game together.
    • Settings integration: use shared rosters and branding from Daily Pick’s Settings page for smoother team setup.

    4. Shared Physics Teamplay

    Momentum Mayhem’s signature feature is the shared physics interaction model. Every click affects the same ball, which encourages teams to coordinate clicks and react to changing trap positions.

    This shared control setup turns a simple browser game into a collaborative team exercise where momentum, timing, and communication matter. It works especially well for remote teams that want a quick, playful way to build alignment.

    5. Sharing and Exporting

    You can share the current session with teammates using the session URL. This makes it easy to start the game together without extra setup or account requirements.

    While Momentum Mayhem does not export game results directly, the captured player order is visible on screen and can be copied manually for announcements, retrospective notes, or team follow-up.

    6. When to Use This Game?

    Momentum Mayhem is perfect when your team needs a quick engagement boost or a cooperative warm-up. It helps reduce decision fatigue, build rapport, and keep meetings lively.

    • Before a standup to energize the team.
    • During retros or team-building sessions to add playful interaction.
    • As an icebreaker for newly formed teams or remote workshops.
    • When you want to reinforce collaboration through shared controls.
    • To break up longer meetings with a short, fun activity.
