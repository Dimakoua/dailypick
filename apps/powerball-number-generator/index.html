    1. What Is the Powerball Number Generator?

    The Powerball Number Generator is a free, browser-based quick pick tool that creates random number combinations matching the official Powerball format: 5 main numbers from 1–69 and 1 Powerball from 1–26. Every pick is statistically equivalent to the quick picks generated by official lottery terminal machines.

    I built this tool because most online lottery generators rely on Math.random(), which is not cryptographically secure and can produce biased results. This generator uses crypto.getRandomValues() with Fisher-Yates shuffling and rejection sampling to eliminate modulo bias — giving you truly fair picks every time.

    Powerball is drawn every Monday, Wednesday, and Saturday. There are 9 prize tiers, and tickets are available in 45 US states plus Washington D.C., Puerto Rico, and the US Virgin Islands.

    3. What Are the Powerball Rules?

    Powerball uses a two-pool format. Players pick 5 main numbers from a pool of 1–69 and 1 Powerball from a separate, independent pool of 1–26. The Powerball is drawn from its own pool, which means it can match one of your main numbers — this is normal and follows the official game rules.

    • Main pool: 5 unique numbers from 1–69 (white balls)
    • Bonus pool: 1 number from 1–26 (red Powerball, drawn separately)
    • Draw days: Monday, Wednesday, Saturday
    • Prize tiers: 9 total
    • Minimum win: Match just the Powerball to win $4
    • Jackpot win: Match all 5 main numbers + the Powerball

    The optional Power Play multiplier (2×–10×) can be purchased to boost non-jackpot prizes. The 10× multiplier is available when the jackpot is under $150 million.

    4. How Does the Number Generator Work?

    Under the hood, this generator uses crypto.getRandomValues() — the same cryptographic random number generator used by browsers for security-sensitive operations — instead of the standard Math.random() function that most websites use.

    For the main numbers, it builds a full pool of 1–69, applies a Fisher-Yates shuffle seeded by cryptographic randomness, and takes the first 5 values. For the Powerball, it draws a separate random number from 1–26. Rejection sampling eliminates modulo bias, ensuring every number in the pool has an equal probability of being selected. Each line is an independent draw with no dependency on previous results.
