1. What is the NHL Team Wheel?

The NHL Team Wheel is a random hockey team picker covering all 32 current NHL franchises. Spin it to find out which team you're cheering for in the playoffs, who you're drafting in a hockey pool, or simply which franchise claims your loyalty when you can't decide for yourself. Four presets cover Cup Contenders, the Eastern Conference, the Western Conference, and the full 32-team league.

2. Who should use this wheel?

New hockey fans who want to pick a team without bias. Casual viewers neutral in the playoffs who want a team to cheer for. Fantasy hockey drafters breaking a tie. Fans visiting a city and wanting to know which local team to check out. Anyone who's moved to a new market and needs the wheel to assign them a franchise. Pub quiz groups settling a "best NHL team" argument neutrally.

3. How it works

Choose a preset — Cup Contenders for playoff-ready picks, Eastern or Western Conference to stay in your half of the league, or All 32 Teams for the full random lottery. Hit Drop the Puck and the wheel spins. Whatever it lands on, that's your team. This is now non-negotiable.

4. Features

  • Four presets: Cup Contenders, Eastern Conference (16 teams), Western Conference (16 teams), and All 32 Teams.
  • All current franchises: Updated to include Utah Hockey Club (formerly Arizona Coyotes).
  • Mobile-friendly: Spin on your phone during a game, at the bar, or in the middle of a hockey argument.
  • Customisable: Load your own list — division rivals, your fantasy pool names, or historical dynasties.
  • Instant result: Your team is displayed clearly the moment the wheel stops.

6. Tips

Use Cup Contenders when you just want to follow a serious playoff race and need to pick a side. Use Eastern or Western Conference when you want to support a team in a specific half of the bracket. Use All 32 Teams for a truly unbiased assignment — the wheel doesn't care if it gives you the Sharks or the Avalanche. Once the wheel picks, commit. Look up their schedule, buy a jersey, add them to your calendar.

Picking a sports team is surprisingly hard — there's too much tribal pressure, geography, family loyalty, and bandwagon guilt wrapped up in the choice.
