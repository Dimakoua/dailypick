1. What is the Madrid Food Wheel?

Madrid Food Wheel is a random restaurant and dish picker built for the capital’s vibrant food culture. It highlights the city’s classic tapas, iconic bocadillos, late-night snacks, and neighborhood dining scenes.

2. Who should use this wheel?

Madrid locals who want a quick meal idea, visitors exploring the city, and anyone overwhelmed by Madrid’s wide dining selection. Spin and let the city choose the next delicious stop.

3. How it works

Select a preset then hit Spin. The wheel lands on a suggestion and announces it. Use it as a meal prompt, then head out or order in. Spin again for another tasty option.

4. Features

  • Four city presets: Madrid Classics, Spanish Tapas, International Eats, and Neighborhood Picks.
  • Real Madrid picks: Every item reflects the city’s authentic dining culture.
  • Mobile-friendly canvas wheel: Spins smoothly on phones, tablets, and desktops.
  • Instant result: Winner appears clearly after each spin.
  • Customisable colours: Use Local Brand Studio to theme the wheel in Madrid-inspired colors.

5. Madrid food categories

Madrid Classics covers the city’s iconic dishes — patatas bravas, bocadillo de calamares, tortilla española, jamón ibérico, churros con chocolate, croquetas, pulpo a la gallega, and paella. Spanish Tapas highlights tapas favorites — gazpacho, salmorejo, pollo al ajillo, pimientos de padrón, ensaladilla rusa, morcilla, migas, and queso manchego. International Eats reflects Madrid’s global dining scene — tacos, ramen, burgers, bánh mì, poke bowls, sushi, Italian pizza, and Greek mezze. Neighborhood Picks points to local districts — Malasaña cafés, La Latina brunch, Chueca desserts, Salamanca steak, Retiro salads, Lavapiés street food, Argüelles burgers, and Plaza Mayor snacks.

6. Tips

Choose Madrid Classics for authentic Spanish comfort food, Spanish Tapas for sharing plates, International Eats for global flavors, and Neighborhood Picks when you want to explore a district. Add your favorite Madrid restaurants to personalize the wheel.

7. Alternatives

For a broader food picker, try What Should I Eat Wheel. If you want a cuisine-specific choice, use the Indian, Chinese, or Japanese food wheels. Need a drink pairing? Try What Drink Should I Choose.

Madrid’s food scene is rich, social, and always buzzing. This wheel turns that abundance into one fast, fun decision.
