1. What is the Montreal Food Wheel?

Montreal Food Wheel is a random restaurant and dish picker built for the city’s vibrant food culture. It highlights the city’s iconic eats — from Montreal bagels and smoked meat to weekend brunch, food trucks, and neighbourhood dining.

2. Who should use this wheel?

Montrealers who want a fast meal decision, visitors exploring the city, and anyone overwhelmed by the many great dining options. Spin and let Montreal choose the next tasty stop.

3. How it works

Pick a preset then hit Spin. The wheel lands on a suggestion and announces it. Use it as a meal prompt, then head out or order in. Spin again for a second delicious idea.

4. Features

  • Four city presets: Montreal Classics, International Eats, Food Truck Favorites, and Neighbourhood Picks.
  • Real Montreal picks: Every item is grounded in the city’s food scene.
  • Mobile-friendly canvas wheel: Spins smoothly on phones, tablets, and desktops.
  • Instant result: Winner appears clearly after each spin.
  • Customisable colours: Use Local Brand Studio to theme the wheel in Montreal palettes.

5. Montreal food categories

Montreal Classics covers the city’s defining dishes — Montreal bagels, smoked meat sandwiches, poutine, steamés, maple crepes, tourtière, croissants, and French toast brunch. International Eats reflects Montreal’s global dining scene — St Viateur coffee, pho, Mile End brunch, sushi, Greek salad, tacos, Indian curry, and Italian pasta. Food Truck Favorites highlights market and street food — food truck poutine, street fries, BeaverTail, bánh mì, crepe stands, doner kebabs, loaded hot dogs, and plant-based bowls. Neighbourhood Picks points to local dining hubs — Plateau cafés, Old Montreal seafood, Little Italy pizza, Griffintown brunch, Quartier Latin tapas, Outremont deli, Westmount bistro, and Verdun burgers.

6. Tips

Choose Montreal Classics for the city’s iconic comfort food, International Eats for global flavours, Food Truck Favorites for casual market dining, and Neighbourhood Picks when you want to explore a local neighbourhood. Add your favourite Montreal spots for a personalised wheel.

7. Alternatives

For a broader meal picker, try What Should I Eat Wheel. If you want a cuisine-specific choice, use the Indian, Chinese, or Japanese food wheels. Need a drink to pair? Try What Drink Should I Choose.

8. Why it works

Montreal’s food scene blends French influence, Quebecois classics, and international flavours. That abundance makes choice hard, and this wheel turns it into one fast, playful decision.
