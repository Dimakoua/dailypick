    1. What Is the UK National Lottery Generator?

    The UK National Lottery Number Generator is a free, browser-based quick pick tool that creates random number combinations matching the official UK Lotto format: 6 main numbers from 1–59 and 1 bonus ball drawn from the remaining pool. Every pick is statistically equivalent to quick picks generated by official lottery terminal machines.

    I built this because most online generators use Math.random(), which isn't cryptographically secure. This tool uses crypto.getRandomValues() with Fisher-Yates shuffling and rejection sampling to eliminate modulo bias — ensuring every number has an equal chance of being selected.

    UK Lotto is the National Lottery's flagship game, drawn every Wednesday and Saturday. The jackpot is guaranteed at £2 million every draw and rolls over when not won. Matching just 2 numbers wins a free Lucky Dip for the next draw.

    3. What Are the UK Lotto Rules?

    UK Lotto uses a same-pool bonus format. Players pick 6 main numbers from a pool of 1–59. After the 6 main numbers are drawn, a bonus ball is drawn from the remaining 53 numbers — guaranteeing it is different from all main numbers. This is a key difference from two-pool lotteries like EuroMillions.

    • Main pool: 6 unique numbers from 1–59
    • Bonus: 1 number from the remaining 53 (same pool, guaranteed distinct)
    • Draw days: Wednesday and Saturday
    • Prize tiers: 6 total
    • Jackpot win: Match all 6 main numbers
    • Second prize: Match 5 main numbers + bonus
    • Free Lucky Dip: Match 2 numbers for a free entry to the next draw

    4. How Does the Number Generator Work?

    This generator uses crypto.getRandomValues() — the same cryptographic random number generator browsers use for security-sensitive operations — instead of Math.random(). It builds a full pool of 1–59, applies a Fisher-Yates shuffle seeded by cryptographic randomness, and takes the first 6 values as main numbers. The bonus number is then drawn from the remaining 53 values. Rejection sampling eliminates modulo bias, and each line is an independent draw.
