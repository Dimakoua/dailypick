1. What is the Toronto Food Wheel?

Toronto Food Wheel is a random restaurant and dish picker built around the real Toronto dining scene. It covers the city's culinary identity — from the iconic peameal bacon sandwich and butter tarts that define Toronto food culture, to the multicultural neighbourhoods that make the 6ix one of the most exciting cities to eat in on earth.

2. Who should use this wheel?

Torontonians who can't pick between a hundred excellent options, visitors trying to experience the city's food scene, and anyone who has stood at Yonge and Dundas paralysed by too many great choices. Spin, walk, eat.

3. How it works

Choose one of the four presets, then hit Spin. The wheel lands on a pick and announces it. Use it as a category prompt — then find the best spot near you for that dish or neighbourhood. Spin again if it lands somewhere too far.

4. Features

  • Four city presets: Toronto Icons, Downtown Eats, Multicultural Mix, and Neighbourhood Gems.
  • Real Toronto picks: Every item is grounded in an actual Toronto food tradition, district, or scene.
  • Mobile-friendly canvas wheel: Spins smoothly on phones, tablets, and desktops — ideal for deciding on the go.
  • Instant result: Winner is shown clearly after every spin so you can act on it fast.
  • Customisable colours: Use Local Brand Studio to theme the wheel in Toronto red.

5. Toronto food categories

Toronto Icons covers the dishes that define the city — the peameal bacon sandwich from St Lawrence Market, butter tarts, poutine, Nanaimo bars, BeaverTails, Timbits, jerk chicken from Eglinton West, and empanadas from Kensington. Downtown Eats reflects the King West and Entertainment District dining scene — sushi, ramen, steakhouses, oyster bars, craft burgers, and rooftop pizza. Multicultural Mix is Toronto at its best — Vietnamese pho on Spadina, West Indian roti in Scarborough, Korean BBQ on Bloor, Sri Lankan curry on Gerrard, dim sum in Markham, Ethiopian injera on Danforth, falafel wraps in Kensington, and Jamaican patties everywhere. Neighbourhood Gems maps the city's distinct food villages — souvlaki on the Danforth, pasta in Little Italy, pierogi on Roncesvalles, butter chicken on Gerrard, dim sum in Chinatown, tacos in Kensington, and fish & chips at the Harbourfront.

6. Tips

Use Toronto Icons for a classic local experience, Multicultural Mix when you want to explore the city's diversity, Downtown Eats for a date night or expense account dinner, and Neighbourhood Gems when you want to pick a destination neighbourhood and wander. Swap in your personal Toronto restaurant favourites for a fully personalised city food wheel.

7. Alternatives

8. Why it works

Toronto has more restaurants per capita than New York City, and a culinary diversity that makes every neighbourhood feel like a different country. That richness is exactly what makes choosing so hard. This wheel turns the city's best food moments into one fast, playful decision. One spin, one meal — let's go.
