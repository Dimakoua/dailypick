Retro Board – collaborative sprint retrospective board for agile teams

Retro Board is a collaborative retrospective tool that helps teams collect insights, vote on priorities, and organize action items. It is built to make sprint retrospectives more structured, visible, and productive.

Use this board to capture what went well, what needs improvement, and action items in a central space that the whole team can interact with.

The tool supports live collaboration, drag-and-drop organization, and clear facilitation workflows.

1. What Is Retro Board?

Retro Board is a sprint retrospective board designed for agile teams to capture feedback, group ideas, and vote on outcomes. The primary keyword is retro board, and the tool helps teams reflect on the last sprint with a clear, shared workspace.

It provides columns for team insights, voting, and action items so your retro stays focused and actionable.

Teams can use it to turn discussion into decisions, with cards that are easy to move, edit, and prioritize.

2. How to Use It?

  1. Open Retro Board and share the session link with your team.
  2. Add cards to reflect on what went well, what could improve, and action items.
  1. Use voting to surface the most important ideas.
  1. Drag and drop cards to organize the board and plan next steps.

3. Key Features & Customization

  • Structured columns: default reflections and action item sections guide your flow.
  • Voting system: prioritize the most important retro feedback quickly.
  • Markdown support: add code snippets, formatting, and richer details to cards.
  • Drag-and-drop: move cards between columns and reorder them easily.
  • Live collaboration: teammates see updates instantly as cards are added or moved.

4. Real-Time Retrospectives

Retro Board is built for live, collaborative retrospectives. Team members can add cards in real time, vote on what matters, and adjust the board without delay.

Seeing changes as they happen helps keep engagement high and ensures feedback is captured while it is fresh.

This makes retros shorter, more actionable, and more aligned with agile improvement cycles.

5. Sharing and Exporting

Share the session link with your team to collaborate live, and use the Copy session or Copy data buttons to preserve the current board state. This makes it easy to document the retrospective or continue the discussion later.

The board’s results can be copied into notes or retrospective reports for follow-up action.

6. When to Use This Retro Tool?

Use Retro Board when you want a structured, collaborative way to run retrospectives. It is especially helpful for agile teams that want to make their reflections actionable.

  • During sprint retrospectives to capture team feedback.
  • For remote or hybrid teams that need live collaboration.
  • When you want a voting-driven retrospective process.
  • To organize improvement ideas and action items visually.
  • When you want a reusable, shareable retrospective session.
