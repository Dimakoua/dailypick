Frequently Asked Questions

What is a random name picker and how does it work?

A random name picker lets you paste or type a list of names, then draws one (or more) at random using a fair, unbiased algorithm. Daily Pick's Random Name Picker is free, browser-based, and requires no sign-up. Teachers use it to call on students, streamers use it for giveaways, and teams use it for assigning tasks.

How do I spin a wheel online for free?

Open the Choices Spinner on Daily Pick, type in your options, and hit spin. The wheel is completely free, works in any browser, and needs no account. You can also try the Yes or No Wheel for quick binary decisions, or one of our themed food wheels if you're stuck on what to eat.

Can I use Daily Pick for classroom activities?

Yes — the Classroom Name Roulette is built specifically for teachers to call on students or assign helpers fairly. The Random Name Picker and Pinball Picker also work great in a classroom setting. All tools run directly in the browser with no logins or installs required.

Does Daily Pick require an account or payment?

No. Every tool on Daily Pick is completely free and works without creating an account. Just open the page and start using it. There are no premium tiers, no sign-up walls, and no hidden costs.

What lottery number generators are available?

Daily Pick covers all major lotteries: Powerball (US), Mega Millions (US), EuroMillions, UK National Lottery, Lotto 6/49 (Canada), Lotto Max (Canada), Daily Grand (Canada), Lucky for Life (US), and Australian Powerball. Each generates a random quick pick in one click.

What stand-up games work well for remote teams?

All of Daily Pick's stand-up games are browser-based, so they work equally well in-person or fully remote — just share your screen. Fan favourites include the Wheel and Speedway turtle race for speaker order, Patchinko Machine for a pachinko-style draw, and Gravity Drift for a space-themed pick. Use Roster to save your team's names across all games.
